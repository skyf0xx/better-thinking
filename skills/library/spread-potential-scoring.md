# Spread-Potential Scoring

Score consumer product/feature ideas meant to spread person-to-person on five multiplicative gates, in one definitive pass with no revision loop.

## Why

Averaging lets one strong dimension mask a fatal weakness — huge payload with no shareable artifact still won't spread. Multiplying makes a single zero kill the idea outright, matching how spread actually fails: one broken link stops the whole loop.

## Use when / Don't use when

- **Use when:** ranking or narrowing consumer product/feature ideas whose success depends on one user showing it to another; evaluating divergence output for virality or organic growth.
- **Don't use when:** ideas are names, B2B/enterprise, internal tools, or have no P2P spread mechanic — say so and offer qualitative discussion instead of forcing the gates. For mixed lists, score only what fits.

## Inputs → Outputs

- **Inputs:** a raw list of already-generated P2P-oriented ideas (don't regenerate or add ideas here).
- **Outputs:** each idea scored and ranked, weakest gate named for every idea outside the top tier.

## Principles

- Multiply the five gate scores, never average — one 0 zeroes the idea, and that's diagnostic, not something to round up.
- Score as a skeptic: realistic base rate for a top score is under 1%; if most of the list scores high, the grading is too generous.
- Don't award a 2 on Transport-Reach or Loop Closure unless the actual artifact can be named.
- A one-shot idea with no retention hook outranks nothing, regardless of raw score — pure novelty spikes and dies without a re-arm mechanism.

## Procedure

1. Confirm scope: every idea needs a plausible P2P spread mechanic (see Don't use when); set aside anything that doesn't fit.
2. Score each idea 0-2 per gate — Payload: solves a live pain / breaks a believed limit / triggers strong emotion (strongest single source, not an average of utility and novelty). Transport-Reach: a nameable artifact travels cold with zero framing. Sender Payoff: sharing pays the sender in status, or costs them something real. Receive Conversion: the 5-second action to a motivated stranger is obvious. Loop Closure: using it automatically produces a fresh sendable artifact.
3. Multiply the five scores per idea (max 32); name the failing gate for any 0.
4. Set two modifiers: Cadence (one-shot/periodic/habitual — what re-arms the sender) and Audience Density (sparse/clustered — does a recipient know 3+ other natural recipients; sparse caps outcome regardless of score).
5. Flag any one-shot idea with no stated retention hook; rank it below ideas that pass this filter even at a lower raw score.
6. Rank by total with the hard filter applied; lead with the top 2-3 and name the weakest gate for every idea outside the top tier.
7. Report residual uncertainty: which scores were genuinely borderline, and whether the list skews from one divergence pass in a way diagnostic of that pass, not just the ideas.

## Common mistakes

- Averaging instead of multiplying, letting a fatal gate hide behind strong scores elsewhere.
- Scoring generously to be encouraging — a flat 0 on a favorite idea is real information.
- Awarding a 2 on Transport-Reach or Loop Closure without being able to name the literal artifact.
- Forcing the rubric onto B2B, naming, or internal-tool ideas where the gates don't mean anything.

## Examples

- Ranking consumer app feature concepts for organic growth potential before a roadmap review.
- Scoring campaign mechanics (referral programs, shareable results pages) for a marketing launch.
- Evaluating which "shareable result" product idea (a quiz, a scorecard, a generated artifact) to build first.
